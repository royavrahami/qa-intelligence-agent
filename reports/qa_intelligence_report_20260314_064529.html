Code-Space Response Oracles: Generating Interpretable Multi-Agent Policies with Large Language Models
🕵️ AI Agents 10 Mar 2026 17:37 UTC
Summary
The paper introduces Code-Space Response Oracles (CSRO), a framework that utilizes Large Language Models (LLMs) to generate interpretable policies in multi-agent reinforcement learning. By reframing policy generation as a code generation task, CSRO replaces traditional deep reinforcement learning oracles with LLMs, resulting in policies that are both interpretable and competitive in performance. The approach leverages LLMs' pretrained knowledge to discover complex strategies and explores methods like zero-shot prompting and iterative refinement to enhance policy generation.
Key Insights
▸ CSRO replaces black-box neural network policies with human-readable code, enhancing interpretability and trust.
▸ Leveraging LLMs in policy generation allows for the discovery of complex, human-like strategies.
▸ CSRO's methods, such as zero-shot prompting and iterative refinement, improve the generation of diverse and explainable policies.

Enhancing Value Alignment of LLMs with Multi-agent system and Combinatorial Fusion
🕵️ AI Agents 11 Mar 2026 14:57 UTC
Summary
The paper introduces the Value Alignment System using Combinatorial Fusion Analysis (VAS-CFA), a novel framework designed to improve the alignment of large language models (LLMs) with human values. Unlike traditional methods that rely on single evaluators, VAS-CFA employs multiple moral agents, each representing different normative perspectives, and combines their outputs through rank- and score-based aggregation. This approach enhances the cognitive diversity of the system, leading to better alignment with ethical pluralism. Empirical results show that VAS-CFA outperforms existing methods in aligning LLMs with human values.
Key Insights
▸ VAS-CFA uses multiple moral agents to represent diverse normative perspectives, enhancing alignment with human values.
▸ Combinatorial Fusion Analysis (CFA) aggregates outputs from these agents, using both rank- and score-based methods to improve decision-making.
▸ Empirical evaluations indicate that VAS-CFA surpasses single-agent and previous aggregation methods in aligning LLMs with ethical pluralism.
💼 Why This Matters for Quality Managers
For QA Managers and Tech Project Managers, understanding VAS-CFA's approach to value alignment is crucial for ensuring that AI systems are ethically sound and meet user expectations. This framework's use of multiple agents and CFA can inform testing strategies that assess the ethical alignment of AI outputs. Additionally, the multi-agent approach can be applied to enhance the robustness of automated testing systems by incorporating diverse perspectives, leading to more comprehensive quality assurance processes.

Adaptive Collaboration with Humans: Metacognitive Policy Optimization for Multi-Agent LLMs with Continual Learning
🕵️ AI Agents 09 Mar 2026 05:18 UTC
Summary
The paper discusses the limitations of purely autonomous multi-agent systems (MAS) due to their reliance on static pre-trained models. It introduces the Human-In-the-Loop Multi-Agent Collaboration (HILA) framework, which integrates human expertise to enhance decision-making in MAS. HILA employs a metacognitive policy that determines when agents should act autonomously or seek human input, using Dual-Loop Policy Optimization to improve both immediate decision-making and long-term learning. Experiments demonstrate that HILA outperforms traditional MAS in complex problem-solving tasks.
Key Insights
▸ Integrate human expertise into MAS to enhance adaptability and performance in novel scenarios.
▸ Utilize Dual-Loop Policy Optimization to separate immediate decision-making from long-term learning improvements.
▸ Implement continual learning to transform human feedback into enhanced agent reasoning capabilities.
💼 Why This Matters for Quality Managers

This approach is crucial for ensuring quality and reliability in software systems that encounter novel or unexpected challenges. Additionally, the continual learning aspect of HILA can be leveraged to enhance testing strategies and improve the overall quality of software delivery processes.
